## Overview
Pheasy is a robust and user-friendly Python program designed for first-principles phonon physics. It accurately reconstructs the potential energy surface of crystalline solids via a Taylor expansion of arbitrarily high order. It enables the efficient and accurate extraction of interatomic force constants (IFCs) from force-displacement datasets and calculates phonon quasiparticle properties.

**Scientific domain**: Lattice dynamics, phonon transport, high-order force constants, anharmonicity
**Target user community**: Materials scientists, condensed matter physicists

## Capabilities
- **IFC Extraction**: Efficient extraction of harmonic and anharmonic interatomic force constants.
- **Potential Energy Surface**: Reconstruction via high-order Taylor expansion.
- **Phonon Properties**: Calculation of phonon dispersion, lifetimes, and thermodynamic properties.
- **Integration**: Works with first-principles force calculators (DFT).

## Inputs & Outputs
- **Inputs**: Structure files, force-displacement datasets (from DFT).
- **Outputs**: Force constants, phonon spectra, thermal properties.
