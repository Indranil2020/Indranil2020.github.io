About Me

I am a P4F Fellow at the Institute of Physics, Czech Academy of Sciences (FZU), working in the Department of Condensed Matter Theory. My research focuses on computational materials science with expertise in Density Functional Theory (DFT), Time-Dependent DFT, and ferroelectric materials.

I completed my Ph.D. in Electronics and Communication Engineering from PDPM IIITDM Jabalpur (2022), where I investigated the theoretical aspects of optoelectronic properties of III-V alloys and quantum confined structures for infrared applications. My research combines first-principles calculations with advanced computational methods to explore novel materials for quantum electronics and neuromorphic computing.

Current Research Project

Title: Molecular Optoelectronics on Ferroelectric Substrates

Duration: January 2025 - January 2027

Supervisor: Ing. Prokop Hapala, Ph.D.

Focus: Exploring hybrid organic-inorganic devices that harness ferroelectric perovskites and molecular switches to advance quantum electronics and neuromorphic computing applications.

Research Interests

Density Functional Theory

First-principles calculations using DFT and TD-DFT for materials characterization and property prediction.

Ferroelectric Materials

Study of ferroelectric perovskites, their substrates, and applications in molecular optoelectronics and shape memory alloys.

Quantum Structures

Theoretical investigation of quantum confined structures, quantum-dot cellular automata (QCA), and quantum computing devices.

Optoelectronics

Research on optoelectronic properties of III-V alloys, infrared photodetector design, and hybrid organic-inorganic devices.

Solar Energy

Excited-state dynamics toolkit combining ab initio and quantum system techniques for solar energy applications.

Neuromorphic Computing

Development of materials and devices for neuromorphic computing using ferroelectric materials and molecular switches.
